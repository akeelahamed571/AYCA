About the System

AYCA Research Project addresses a pressing issue that has long plagued legal professionals and students in Sri Lanka. The laborious and time-consuming nature of legal research, argument preparation, and study material discovery has posed a significant challenge to lawyers and law students alike. This often intricate and exhaustive process can be overwhelming when dealing with legal cases in Sri Lanka. Recognizing this problem, our dedicated team of upcoming data science graduates embarked on this ambitious venture, committed to revolutionizing the way legal information is accessed and comprehended. Leveraging cutting-edge technology and innovative solutions, AYCA Research Project aims to streamline and simplify the intricate world of law, making it accessible and efficient for everyone involved. Our core components tackle these obstacles in a sequential manner: First, we employ a custom Named Entity Recognition (NER) model to precisely identify and extract key legal entities in Supreme court cases, which form the backbone of our comprehensive legal knowledge base, depicted in our knowledge graph. This knowledge graph represents the intricate relationships between multiple legal cases, offering an extensive understanding of legal precedents. Subsequently, our legal case summarization feature, powered by BART, condenses extensive legal documents into concise and insightful summaries. Finally, our legal question-answering component, driven by RoBERTa models, provides accurate answers extracted from official documents related to the Narcotic Drug Act in Sri Lanka. In essence, AYCA Research Project is dedicated to streamlining the legal landscape, making legal information readily accessible and efficient for legal practitioners and enthusiasts alike.

Named Entity Recognition (NER)

Custom NER models accurately identify and extract legal entities from Supreme Court verdicts, forming the foundation for AYCA's knowledge graph and improving information retrieval in the legal domain.

Knowledge Graph (Legal Knowledgebase)

AYCA utilizes a knowledge graph to represent complex relationships between legal entities and cases in Sri Lanka's Supreme Court verdicts, enabling a comprehensive and interconnected legal Knowledgebase

Legal Case Summarization

AYCA's legal case summarization feature, powered by BART, condenses lengthy legal documents into concise and insightful summaries, aiding legal professionals and students in their research

Legal Question-Answering (Q&A)

AYCA's question-answering component, utilizing RoBERTa models, provides precise answers extracted from official documents related to the Narcotic Drug Act in Sri Lanka, facilitating quick access to legal information.

LITERATURE SURVEY

The law could be defined as the backbone of modern society. Humans have created the rule of law to bring order to the chaotic world. The legal framework is the structure that makes human society a civilized one. Legal systems and the legal framework have evolved throughout civilization all around the world. With the modernization of technology, the evolution of the legal system has also happened rapidly and drastically.

The legal domain is vastly dependent on documentation and information. As stated above it is evident that technological modernization has affected the entire world, thus, it has directly affected the legal domain too. Due to the advancements in technology, it has become very easy to create and output legal documents and information. Therefore, the legal systems have managed to produce a plethora of information in various forms of documents in recent decades. The above has created a counterproductive aspect of information utilization. Due to the vast amount of available information, information retrieval and processing have become extremely difficult. This has been discussed in an article in the Harvard Business Review by the author Paul Hemp. It has become evident that, with technological advancements, information availability has become very abundant. This plethora of information has surpassed the human brain’s capacity to comprehend and absorb information within a person’s lifetime.

The above phenomenon is a universal issue. When we look at the Sri Lankan context a study has been conducted by the author Aquinas V. Tambimuttu about the Sri Lankan legal system. In this study, the author highlights six main types of courts in Sri Lanka: the Supreme Court, the Court of Appeal, the High Court, the District courts, the Magistrate's courts, and the Primary courts. These court systems have been established to enforce the laws created by the Parliament of Sri Lanka. The Lankan legal system is a mix of several laws and is unique to the country of Sri Lanka. It is a concoction of English common law, Roman-Dutch Law, Kandyan Law, Thesawalamai Law, and Muslim Law. Thus, it is evident that the mixture of laws and the legal infrastructure of the country produces a vast volume of legal information constantly, which redirects to the problem highlighted above. Thus, the need for a proper legal information retrieval system could be identified.

The inability, lack of access, and lack of legal knowledge is the main problem for socio- cultural issues. Through a study done by the authors R. O. Moorthage and H. P. K. N. Hewawasam in the Rathnapura Police Division it has been uncovered, the impact of legal literacy on crime control and prevention. Through this study, the authors have discovered that citizens with the least knowledge about the legal framework, rights, and punishments are the ones who are mostly involved in illicit activities. Ignorance of the rule of law is also a major cause of becoming victims of illegal activities. Thus, it is evident that having proper access to legal knowledge could be beneficial for a civilized society.

The legal domain and access to legal information have become tedious tasks for legal professionals and law students too. These parties are highly dependent on the management of the ever-increasing legal knowledge base, and it affects the productivity and efficiency of these personnel. The importance of legal information management and its effects have been studied by the authors Nina Evans and James Price. Through their studies, it is evident that the proper management of legal information is very important, and this would enable legal professionals to uphold the law to a higher standard. Another study by the Sri Lankan author H.M.P.P Karunarathna has identified that a majority of law students use electronic means of accessing legal information, but out of this, a vast majority suffer due to the unavailability of consistent, accurate, and proper legal information systems. Thus, it is evident that there is an issue in legal information management, information retrieval, and the availability of a proper legal knowledge base.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The increasing number of legal papers in Sri Lanka poses a complex and varied challenge within the legal domain. The matter at hand contains various interrelated facets, all of which contribute to the intricate nature and importance of the issue.

Within the legal framework of Sri Lanka, various entities assume crucial functions, encompassing government offices, courts, law firms, and corporations. The precise identification and extraction of these items are crucial for improving search accuracy and enabling thorough comprehension.

The inclusion of a Legal Document Summarization function is of utmost importance in the advancement of a legal information retrieval system in Sri Lanka. The utilization of automatic summarizing can greatly improve the usability and accessibility of legal material, considering the typically extensive and intricate character of legal documents. In Sri Lanka, the abundance of legal documents such as judgments, legislation, and regulations poses a challenge for legal practitioners and the general populace in effectively extracting pertinent information. The methodologies used for summarizing documents should be customized in order to effectively capture the essential elements of the content while preserving the important legal principles, precedent cases, and legal reasoning. Another issue that arises is the absence of legal knowledge hubs in existing systems. Legal ontologies play a fundamental role in comprehending the semantic framework of legal data, hence facilitating accurate retrieval of legal information in the context of Sri Lanka. The development of comprehensive legal ontologies that are specifically designed for the legal system of Sri Lanka is a substantial research challenge.

A survey was undertaken among members of the legal community, specifically legal professionals such as law students and lawyers, to identify the unique issues they face in their day-to-day professional activities. The survey results have provided significant insights into the urgent needs within the field of law. The participants emphasized the importance of a comprehensive legal information retrieval system that would facilitate their access to legal materials. The implementation of this technology would significantly augment the efficacy of legal research and the process of case preparation.

RESEARCH GAP

In considering recent advancements in modern technology, individuals have initiated an exploration for resolutions to issues arising from the utilization of modern technology and systems. This trend has revealed itself within the legal sector as well. The increasing amount of information within the legal sector has become a significant phenomenon, prompting individuals to develop diverse methods aimed at addressing these challenges.

There have been notable advancements in the area of legal information retrieval, indicating a substantial body of research conducted globally in the field of legal information retrieval. The syntaxes and semantics of legal information and documents vary across different countries worldwide. The variation in cultural, traditional, geopolitical, and international influences experienced by different countries and their legal systems leads to differences in the concepts, vocabulary, and structure of legal information across jurisdictions. The primary research need observed is the lack of a legal information retrieval system specifically designed to address the unique requirements of the Sri Lankan legal landscape. Based on the conducted literature survey, it was determined that the systems developed primarily prioritize the legal systems specific to the corresponding country. Based on an extensive review of existing literature and a comprehensive analysis of existing systems, it is evident that there are significant gaps that can be addressed through the construction of a Legal Information Retrieval System, as emphasized in this research.

MAIN OBJECTIVE

The implemented legal information retrieval system, AYCA aims to provide a user- friendly and multifaceted solution for retrieving legal information in the Sri Lankan legal context that is efficient, accurate, and consistent. The implemented system has made it easier for legal experts to make informed decisions by analyzing legal case reports and providing decision support. It has been a valuable resource for both lawyers and laypeople in Sri Lanka seeking legal information. Furthermore, the system has allowed users to locate all relevant information related to the case or information sought in legal case reports.
The implemented system has fulfilled the main objective and has addressed the lack of legal information by providing consistent access to such documents, thereby revolutionizing Sri Lanka's legal industry. Its time-saving legal information search capabilities have benefited both lawyers and law students, resulting in higher legal industry standards in the country.

Specific Objectives
Data Extraction and Structuring
This specific goal of this study is to tackle the challenges posed by disorganized legal documents in Sri Lanka. It does this by creating a structured framework within a larger system called "AYCA," which is a Legal Information Retrieval System in Sri Lanka. This framework aims to make it easier for legal professionals to find important information in legal documents quickly, saving them time when dealing with large amounts of text. By organizing legal documents and using advanced techniques to find information, this research aims to make legal materials more accessible and useful, ultimately making life easier for legal practitioners.

Legal Knowledge Graph Development & Document Retrieval
This research also aims to build a strong legal knowledge graph specifically designed for Sri Lanka. This graph will be focused on Supreme Court Verdicts and will serve as the foundation for a comprehensive legal information retrieval system. By implementing this knowledge graph, it will become much easier for legal professionals, including lawyers, practitioners, and students, to search, retrieve, and review legal case reports and related documents within Sri Lanka's legal system. They will have advanced search options, such as filtering by case numbers, parties involved, dates, judges, and legal citations. Ultimately, this will make legal information more relevant and accessible.

Legal Document Summarization
This section explains the specific goal, the legal document summarizer. The goal is to help lawyers, law students, and the general public, who were identified as key users, quickly understand lengthy legal case documents. To achieve this, the summarizer must create very short yet accurate summaries that capture the most important parts of the document. To ensure accuracy and relevance, these summaries need to be validated by a legal expert because even if the model is accurate, it might miss crucial details. So, the main objective here is to provide users with brief, accurate summaries that include the essential information they need from the document, as requested.

Legal Question-Answering
Another sub-objective of this research is to create a sophisticated legal decision support system customized for Sri Lankan laws. This system centers on an advanced question- answering mechanism, aiming to provide accurate legal answers to users, including legal professionals and students. It uses cutting-edge technology, specifically a transformer- based approach, which uses advanced natural language processing and machine learning to understand complex legal language and deliver precise responses. This research has a dual purpose: bridging the gap between Sri Lankan legal documents and accessible legal knowledge and simplifying legal research to provide quicker and more reliable answers to legal questions.

METHODOLOGY

The methodology employed in this research project has been meticulously designed to tackle the goal of implementing a systematic legal information retrieval system for Sri Lankan Supreme Court judgments and laws, with the aim of enhancing the accurate retrieval of legal information. The Sri Lankan Legal Information Retrieval System serves as a comprehensive knowledge base within the Sri Lankan legal framework, specifically emphasizing Supreme Court verdicts and the laws pertaining to the Narcotic Drug Act. The present methodology is organized into distinct stages, each of which assumes a critical function in the advancement of the system and the retrieval of relevant case documents, case summaries, and responses to user questions. During the process of this research, the methodology employed adhered to an agile development approach, which effectively enables flexibility and gradual improvements. In the Sri Lankan legal domain, the utilization of a user-friendly web application interface that incorporates the knowledge graph holds the potential to enhance the experience of end users. This interface would facilitate efficient and accurate searching and retrieval of case information. Additionally, the integration of the BART model for case summary generation and the RoBERTa model for question answering further enhances the functionality of the application. Ultimately, these advancements aim to benefit end users by providing them with a more streamlined and effective means of accessing and utilizing legal information.

AYCA - We Democratize Law

AYCA is a legal AI system that utilizes a knowledge graph, legal case summarization, named entity recognition, and legal question-answering to provide a comprehensive and interconnected legal database, concise and insightful legal case summaries, accurate information retrieval from Supreme Court verdicts, and precise answers to legal questions related to the Narcotic Drug Act in Sri Lanka.

Reduced time spent preparing arguments for cases (lawyers)
AYCA's comprehensive and interconnected legal database, concise and insightful legal case summaries, and accurate information retrieval features can help lawyers reduce the time they spend preparing arguments for cases. By providing easy access to relevant legal information and insights, AYCA can help lawyers develop more informed and persuasive arguments.

Reduced time spent studying and getting an overview of cases (law students)
AYCA's legal case summarization feature and question-answering component can help law students reduce the time they spend studying and getting an overview of cases. By providing concise and insightful summaries of lengthy legal documents and quick and precise answers to legal questions, AYCA can help law students learn more efficiently and effectively.

Understanding their legal rights (General Public)
The Q&A feature can help people to understand their legal rights under the Narcotic Drug Act in Sri Lanka. For example, people can ask questions about the laws governing drug possession, use, and trafficking. This information can help people to avoid violating the law and to protect their rights.
